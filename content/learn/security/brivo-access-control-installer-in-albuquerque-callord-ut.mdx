The platform's strength is its cloud architecture — administrators manage doors, users, and schedules from anywhere through the Onramp portal. There's no on-premise server to maintain. For multi-site organizations, Brivo scales cleanly across locations under a single tenant.

## System Architecture Overview

A typical Brivo installation includes:

**ACS Controller** — The panel that manages door readers, locks, and events. The ACS300 handles up to 8 doors per controller; the ACS600 series scales higher. Controllers connect to the cloud via Ethernet or cellular backup.

**Readers** — Brivo supports multiple reader technologies: proximity, card swipe, keypad, and mobile credential readers. Reader choice depends on your security tier and user experience goals.

**Mobile Credentialing** — Users authenticate via the Brivo Mobile app instead of physical cards. Mobile credentials eliminate lost-card scenarios and enable instant revocation.

**Onramp Platform** — The cloud portal where administrators configure doors, assign credentials, set schedules, and review audit logs. Accessible from any browser.

## Common Deployment Scenarios

**Single-Door Entry** — One ACS300 controller, one reader, one lock. Typical for small office entries, retail storefronts, or equipment rooms.

**Multi-Door Facility** — Multiple ACS300 or ACS600 controllers wired to a central network. Common in office buildings, medical facilities, and educational campuses.

**Multi-Site Portfolio** — One Brivo tenant managing dozens of locations. Ideal for franchises, property management firms, and regional operations.

**Elevator Integration** — Brivo Lift restricts elevator floor access by credential. Essential for high-rise security and tenant privacy.
