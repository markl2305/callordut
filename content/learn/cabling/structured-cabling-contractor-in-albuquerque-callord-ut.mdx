## What Structured Cabling Covers

Structured cabling is the organized network of cables and hardware that carries voice, data, video, and building-control traffic throughout a facility. When done right, it's the invisible layer everything else depends on — and when done wrong, it's the reason your access control drops frames and your conferencing rooms lag.

### The Five Subsystems

| Subsystem | What it does | Typical media |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| Entrance Facilities | Where ISP circuits enter the building | Fiber, coax |
| Equipment Room | Core switching and patching | Rack-mounted gear |
| Backbone Cabling | Inter-floor and inter-building links | Fiber, Cat6A |
| Horizontal Cabling | Drops to individual workstations | Cat6 / Cat6A |
| Work Area | Wall jacks, patch cords, devices | Patch cables |

A proper design documents all five subsystems before a single cable gets pulled — because fixing a bad horizontal run after drywall goes up costs ten times what planning it correctly would have.

## Why Most Cabling Jobs Go Wrong

We've seen enough botched installations to name the pattern:

1. **No pre-install survey.** Installers who skip the site walk discover conduit blockages and ceiling obstructions mid-job — and bill you for the surprise.
2. **Mixed standards.** Some drops Cat5e, some Cat6, fiber spliced inconsistently. The network works until it doesn't, and nobody knows why.
3. **No documentation.** The installer leaves, the labels fall off, and troubleshooting becomes archaeology.
4. **Cabling treated as commodity.** It isn't. The same cable that carries your email also carries your access control, your fire alarm, and your surveillance feeds.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does structured cabling cost per drop?

Costs vary by building type, ceiling height, and whether conduit already exists. A rough industry baseline runs $150–$350 per drop for Cat6 in standard office conditions, but your actual number depends on the site. Get a site-specific quote — it's free and it replaces guesswork with a real number.

### Can I upgrade from Cat5e to Cat6 without rewiring?

Not really. Cat6 has stricter bend-radius and separation requirements, and it won't perform reliably on old pathways. The honest answer is a full horizontal replacement, which is cheaper than living with a half-upgraded network for five years.

### How long does a typical office cabling job take?

For a 50-workstation office with existing conduit: roughly two to three weeks from design sign-off to certification. New conduit adds time. We give you a schedule at the design stage, not after the fact.

### Do you handle the ISP circuit installation too?

We coordinate with your ISP and design the entrance facilities to match whatever they bring in. We don't provision the ISP circuit itself — that's their job — but we make sure the infrastructure inside the building is ready the moment the line arrives.

### What happens after the install?

You get a certification report, labeled patch panels, and documentation that travels with the building. We stay reachable for support afterward — because the day something breaks is the worst day to realize there's no one to call.
