## What is structured cabling?

Structured cabling is the organized backbone of your building's network — the cables, pathways, and hardware that carry voice, video, and data. Done right, it supports everything from security cameras to VoIP phones to Wi-Fi access points on a single, documented infrastructure. Done wrong, it's a tangle you'll pay to fix later.

We're CalLord Unified Technologies, an Albuquerque-based commercial security and AV integration firm. We design and install structured cabling systems for offices, multifamily buildings, hospitality properties, and senior living facilities — in New Mexico and nationwide.

## Why structured cabling matters for your building

Most network problems trace back to the cabling layer. Slow connections, dropped cameras, unreliable access control — they look like device failures until you find the real culprit: bad cable runs, unshielded copper near electrical lines, or patch panels nobody labeled.

A proper structured cabling system fixes all three at once:

**Reliability.** Shielded Cat6A and multimode fiber carry clean signals across large floor plans without interference.

**Scalability.** Standardized pathways mean adding a new access point or camera takes minutes instead of tearing up ceilings.

**Documentability.** Every run gets mapped and labeled, so whoever inherits the system knows exactly how it works.

## Our structured cabling services

### Cat6 and Cat6A copper cabling

Cat6 handles standard office networking up to 1 Gbps; Cat6A extends that to 10 Gbps over longer distances and is the default choice for modern builds. We pull both, always keeping copper runs away from electrical lines to prevent interference.

### Fiber optic cabling

Multimode fiber for campus backbones and long-distance runs where copper won't reach. Fiber carries far more bandwidth over much greater distances — essential for multi-building sites.

### Access control wiring

Card readers, door strikes, and controllers need clean, shielded runs to their panels. We route access control cabling alongside the main backbone so the two systems stay coordinated.

### Camera network cabling

Security cameras draw power and data over the same cable (PoE), which makes cable quality matter even more. Bad cabling shows up as flickering feeds and dropped recordings.

### Patch panel and rack organization

The termination point is where most cabling jobs go sloppy. We terminate every run at a labeled patch panel with a documented port map — the kind of finish that lets someone else maintain the system years down the road.

## The process

1. **Site survey.** We walk the space, map existing pathways, and identify where new runs need to go.
2. **Design.** Cable routes, equipment locations, and a bill of materials.
3. **Installation.** Pulling cable, terminating ends, labeling every run.
4. **Testing and certification.** Signal quality verified on every run, with a report you keep.
5. **Documentation.** A network map and port directory handed off with the job.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does structured cabling cost?

It depends on square footage, number of drops, and whether you're retrofitting an existing building or running cable in a new construction. We give you a fixed bid after the site survey — no hourly surprises.

### How long does a typical cabling project take?

A small office (under 50 drops) usually wraps in a few days. Larger commercial spaces run a week or more depending on ceiling access and coordination with other trades.

### Will I lose internet during installation?

No. We phase work so your live network stays up, or we schedule heavy pulling during off-hours. You decide the pace.

### Do you certify the cabling afterward?

Yes. Every run gets tested and a certification report delivered with the job. That report is also what warranty claims rest on, so it's worth having.

### Can you work around my existing network?

Absolutely. Most cabling jobs happen while the building is occupied, and we plan around your live systems rather than asking you to shut them down.
