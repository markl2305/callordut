## A Q-SYS integrator serving the Southwest

CalLord Unified Technologies is a certified Q-SYS integrator headquartered in Albuquerque, New Mexico, serving clients across New Mexico, Arizona, Colorado, and Texas. We design, program, install, and support Q-SYS audio, video, and control systems for conference rooms, council chambers, and multi-site organizations — full lifecycle delivery from discovery through ongoing support, not hardware resale.

## Why Q-SYS for your next AV deployment

Q-SYS is the industry-standard platform for integrated audio, video, and control in commercial environments. It consolidates DSP, video routing, and touch-panel control into a single software-defined system, which means fewer devices to manage, faster troubleshooting, and a unified interface for operators. For organizations running multiple meeting spaces or campuses, Q-SYS scales from a single room to enterprise-wide deployments managed from one console.

## What CalLord delivers on a Q-SYS engagement

### Discovery and design

We start with how your space actually gets used — who meets there, what they share, how remote participants join, and what happens when someone walks out of the room. That informs the Q-SYS architecture: core placement, codec selection, microphone strategy, and control surface design. Every design ships with documentation that survives leadership turnover, so the next person who touches the system knows what exists and why.

### Programming and installation

Q-SYS Core units are configured and tested before they ever reach the job site. On-site work focuses on physical layer quality — cable termination, device mounting, acoustic treatment coordination — because even the cleanest design fails on a sloppy install. Commissioning includes full-system testing against a checklist that covers every input, output, and control path.

### Training and handoff

Operators get a short, practical session on the control surfaces they'll actually use — not a technical deep-dive. Documentation includes system diagrams, login credentials, and escalation paths, so day-one support doesn't require us on the line.

### Ongoing support

Post-installation support covers firmware updates, configuration changes, troubleshooting, and training refreshers. Remote support resolves most issues without a truck roll; on-site visits are reserved for what genuinely needs them.
